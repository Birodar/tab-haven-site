The best OneTab alternative: save and restore your tabs

How to save, restore and organize your Chrome tabs, so a full window is never lost again.

OneTab has been the go-to way to deal with tab overload for years: click it, and all your open tabs collapse into a single list. It is simple and it frees memory. But if you have used it for a while, you have probably hit its limits: there is no search, no folders, no sync, and if your list ever gets corrupted or wiped, there is no backup. Here is how to save and restore your Chrome tabs properly, and what a good OneTab alternative should do.

The native options, and where they fall short

  • Bookmark all tabs (Ctrl+Shift+D) saves the current tabs into a bookmark folder. It works, but it clutters your bookmarks and there is no easy "restore this whole set" button.
  • Tab groups help you organize live tabs by color and name, but they do not really save a session you can bring back later, and they can disappear after a crash or update.
  • "Continue where you left off" reopens your last session on startup, but only the last one, and only if the browser closes cleanly. Recently closed tabs are capped and easy to lose.

In short, Chrome can save tabs, but not in a way that is reliable, searchable and organized.

What a good OneTab alternative should do

  • Save any set of tabs and restore it later, exactly as it was.
  • Let you search across everything you saved, not just scroll a long list.
  • Organize saved sessions into folders.
  • Protect you from losing data, with automatic backups and a way to recover after a crash.
  • Import your existing OneTab list so you do not lose what you already saved.

The bottom line

OneTab is great at collapsing tabs, but a modern tab manager should also search, organize, back up and recover your sessions. If you keep a lot of tabs open and have ever lost a window you needed, that reliability is worth having.
